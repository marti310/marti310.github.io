My Math Family

The following people have influenced my life during my career. I am thankful to have met all of them.

Friends

The summer prior to my first semester at Stockton University, I attended the EOF program in 2017. This program gives low-income, underprivileged students the opportunity to succeed in college. During this summer academy, students get a head start by taking two classes designed to prepare them for their first year of college.

I met Mimi during EOF 2017. She has supported me since we became friends. I am thankful to have her in my life because she helps me grow and cheers me up whenever I am feeling down :)
Finding friends when you take part of a long journey is key. I am thankful to have them in my life; they fill my life with joy.

During the summer of 2019, I participated at the Mathematical Sciences Research Institute Undergraduate Program (MSRI-UP) at Berkeley, California.
Dr. Pamela E. Harris was the first Latina professor I met. She has inspired my math career. We both speak Spanish and we share more in common.
I met Dr. Rebecca E. Garcia at the 2019 MSRI-UP summer program; she was the on-site director. I thank her for all her advice and mentorship she has provided me. This picture was taken at the 2019 SACNAS conference.
